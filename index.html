Overview

SHA-8192 is a cryptographic hash function in the SHA family that produces a 8192-bit (1024-byte) message digest. It extends the design principles of SHA-512 to dramatically larger state sizes, offering an enormous security margin against both classical and quantum computing attacks.

Hash functions are foundational building blocks of modern cryptography — used to verify data integrity, store passwords securely, and authenticate messages. SHA-8192 pushes the boundaries of digest size, making brute-force and collision attacks computationally infeasible even for adversaries equipped with large-scale quantum computers.

Avalanche Effect
